📖 Fourier Series Theory

Definition: A Fourier series represents a periodic function f(x) with period 2L as an infinite sum of sines and cosines:

f(x) = a₀/2 + Σ [aₙ cos(nπx/L) + bₙ sin(nπx/L)]

Coefficients: aₙ = (1/L)∫ f(x)cos(nπx/L) dx, bₙ = (1/L)∫ f(x)sin(nπx/L) dx

Dirichlet Conditions ensure convergence. Gibbs phenomenon – ringing near jumps.

Examples: Square wave uses only odd sine harmonics; sawtooth includes all sine terms; triangle wave decays as 1/n².
